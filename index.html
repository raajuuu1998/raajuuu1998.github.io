About

I am a researcher working at the intersection of deep learning and biomedical imaging. My primary focus is on computational pathology, where I develop automated methods for analyzing whole slide images of brain tumors — particularly necrosis detection in meningioma and glioblastoma.

Master's Thesis

DeepNucleiNet: Utilizing Domain Adaptation for Deep Learning Based Necrosis Detection in Meningioma and Glioblastoma

Developed a domain-adaptive CNN framework that leverages nuclei structure, density, and sparsity as discriminative features for necrosis detection in brain tumor whole slide images. The model prioritizes necrosis-relevant morphological cues while suppressing irrelevant tissue characteristics. Improved detection accuracy by 35% over baseline, detected necrotic regions as small as 997 µm², and achieved 97% accuracy on Glioblastoma and Low-Grade Glioma despite being trained exclusively on Meningioma data.
